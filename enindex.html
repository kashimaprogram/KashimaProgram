About Kashima Program

This program is a part of the "Kashima Program", which is a municipal collaboration project between Saga University and Kashima City, Saga Prefecture. The first activity of the program, "Connecting Kashima's Attractions to Inbound Travel," started in 2021. With the help of Saga University students, the program aims to convey the attractions of Kashima City both domestically and internationally and connect it to inbound travel.

Attractions of Kashima City

The city is located in the south of Saga Prefecture. Major tourist attractions include the Yutoku Inari Shrine, known as one of the three most important inari shrines in Japan, the Hizen Hamashuku sake brewery street, and the Ariake Sea mud flats, famous for the Gatalympics.

Description of activities

The first（2021.10 → 2021.11）

Production of videos and posters to convey the attractions of Kashima

A mixed group of international and Japanese students from Saga University visited major sightseeing spots in Kashima City for two days and one night, and produced posters and videos including catch phrases that describe the attractions of these spots.
